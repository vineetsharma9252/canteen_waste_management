Introduction to Food Waste Management

Food waste at IIT Guwahati’s Kapili Hostel averages 64.47 kg daily, contributing to environmental issues like methane emissions and financial strain. Effective food waste management involves reducing waste at the source, segregating leftovers, and converting waste into biogas or compost. This guide helps hostel residents adopt sustainable practices.

Types of Food Waste

  • Plate Waste: Uneaten food left on plates, often due to large portions (e.g., 59.57 kg at breakfast daily).
  • Kitchen Waste: Vegetable peels, rice, and dal scraps from meal preparation.
  • Expired Food: Unused food past its shelf life, often due to overstocking.
  • Non-Compostable Waste: Packaging or contaminated food items that cannot be composted.

Food Waste Reduction Tips

Reducing and managing food waste at Kapili Hostel is key to sustainability. Here are practical tips:

  • Pre-Book Meals: Use the hostel’s pre-booking website to notify kitchen staff of your meal needs, reducing overproduction.
  • Take Smaller Portions: Opt for smaller servings and use tasting portions to avoid plate waste.
  • Segregate Waste: Place food waste in designated bins with weighing machines to track and minimize waste.
  • Support Composting: Ensure compostable waste (e.g., rice, vegetables) is separated for biogas or fertilizer production.
  • Follow JIT Practices: Support the Just-in-Time model by confirming meal attendance via RFID cards to optimize food preparation.
