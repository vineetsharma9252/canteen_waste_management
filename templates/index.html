Just-in-Time (JIT) Model

The Just-in-Time (JIT) model ensures materials are received only as needed for production, minimizing inventory and ensuring fresh supplies. Popularized by Toyota in the 1970s as the Toyota Production System, it relies on precise demand forecasting, reliable suppliers, and a culture of continuous improvement. In hostel mess management, JIT aligns with pre-booking to optimize food preparation and reduce waste.

Advantages of JIT in Mess Management

  • Lower Costs & Better Cash Flow: Reduces storage costs by avoiding excess stockpiling, freeing up resources for other needs.
  • Ensures Items Are In-Stock: Timely deliveries based on pre-booking ensure food availability, improving satisfaction and reducing lead times.
  • Improved Efficiency: Streamlined processes and smaller batches minimize waiting times and enable quick response to demand changes.
  • Reduced Waste: Faster stock turnover prevents spoilage, saving money by avoiding unnecessary food preparation.
  • Smoother Production Cycles: Pull-based systems ensure food is prepared based on actual demand, reducing overproduction.
  • Better Quality: Smaller inventory volumes allow better quality control, enabling early detection of issues and consistent food quality.
  • Enhanced Student Discipline: Encourages students to plan meals, fostering timely eating habits and reducing skipped meals.

Biodegradable and Biogas Solutions

Converting food waste into valuable resources:

Food waste is a valuable renewable energy source , contributing to environmental sustainability . It can be also used to make high value organic products like fertilizers and enzymes .

  • High organic content (70-90%) ideal for biogas production, yielding 300-800 liters per kg of volatile solids with 50-70% methane.
  • Production of organic fertilizers and enzymes, supporting sustainable agriculture.
  • Biodegradable waste processed to reduce landfill impact and promote eco-friendly disposal.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does the pre-booking system work?

Students can log in to the system and pre-book their meals for the next day. The mess receives this information and prepares only the required quantity of food. If you skip a meal you've booked, you'll receive a penalty after three strikes.

What happens if I forget to pre-book my meal?

We keep a small buffer for unexpected guests, but priority and guaranteed portions are given to those who pre-book. We encourage all students to make pre-booking a daily habit.

How does the self-weighing station help reduce waste?

The weighing stations provide immediate feedback on how much food you're wasting. This visual and quantitative data helps students become more mindful of their portion sizes and consumption habits.

What happens to the food waste that's collected?

All collected food waste is either composted or sent to biogas plants where it's converted into renewable energy. Nothing goes to landfill.
