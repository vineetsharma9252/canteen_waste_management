Frequently Asked Questions - Food Waste Reduction

Why is food waste a problem at IIT Guwahati's hostels?
Food waste at Kapili Hostel averages 59.57 kg for breakfast, 68.14 kg for lunch, and 65.71 kg for dinner daily. This contributes to environmental issues like methane emissions and financial strain, while 189.2 million people in India face hunger.
What is the Just-in-Time (JIT) model for mess management?
The JIT model, inspired by Toyota, involves preparing food based on pre-booked student orders to minimize waste. It reduces storage needs, improves food quality, and ensures efficient resource use through accurate demand forecasting.
How can students help reduce food waste?
Students can pre-book meals via a website, use RFID cards to track attendance, take smaller portions, and dispose of plate waste in designated bins with weighing machines to monitor and reduce their waste.
What happens to the food waste generated?
Food waste can be converted into biogas (300-800 liters per kg of volatile solids), organic fertilizers, or enzymes. Composting and partnerships with local communities can also redistribute unserved food to address hunger.
